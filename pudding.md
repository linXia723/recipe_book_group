For the custard:

2 cups heavy cream (or whipping cream)

½ cup whole milk

½ cup granulated sugar

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

4 large egg yolks

1 pinch salt

For the caramelized topping:

4 tablespoons granulated sugar (about 1 tablespoon per serving)

Instructions
Prepare the oven and baking dish:

Heat the oven to 150°C (300°F).

Place 4 small ramekins (oven-safe cups) in a deep baking dish.

Make the custard mixture:

In a small pot, heat the heavy cream, milk, and half of the sugar (¼ cup) over medium heat. Stir often until it is hot and tiny bubbles appear around the edge. Do not let it boil.

Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks, the remaining sugar (¼ cup), and the salt until smooth.

Combine and strain:

Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks while whisking constantly. This keeps the eggs from cooking too fast.

Pour the mixture through a fine-mesh strainer into a large measuring cup or bowl. This removes any lumps.

Bake in a water bath:

Pour the custard mixture evenly into the ramekins.

Pour hot water into the baking dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins.

Carefully place the dish in the oven. Bake for 30–40 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center still wobbles a little when you gently shake a ramekin.

Take the ramekins out of the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Then cover and put them in the fridge for at least 4 hours (or overnight).

Add the caramel topping (right before serving):

Sprinkle about 1 tablespoon of sugar evenly over the top of each cold custard.

Use a kitchen torch to melt the sugar. Move the flame in circles until the sugar turns golden brown and hard.

Wait 1 minute for the caramel to harden, then serve right away.
